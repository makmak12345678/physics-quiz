SPEED
Speed is a type of rate. Rate is the amount of something that occurs or changes in one unit of time. If you know the distance an object travels in a certain amount of time, you can calculate the speed of the object. The speed of an object is the distance the object travels per unit of time. Most of the time we measure average speed, not instantaneous speed. Speed commonly recorded in Kilometers per hour.

 Formula
 Speed = distance time in meters in seconds s (m/s) = d (m) / t (s) s = d / t

 Calculating Speed.
 How is speed calculated? Speed = Distance/ Time (s = d/t) To calculate the speed you divide the distance traveled by the time it takes to travel that distance. Distance = speed x time Used to measure how fast plates are moving. UNITS: Measured in m/s.

» Constant Speed- speed does not change over time. Most objects do not move at constant speeds.
» Average Speed- speed of motion when speed is changing. To find it divide total distance by total time. Avg Speed = Total Distance/ Total Time Total distance = 32km + 13km = 45km Total time = 2hr + 1hr = 3hr Average speed = 45km/3hr = 15km per hour » Instantaneous Speed- rate at which an object is moving at any given instant in time (speedometer)

 Example
 Calculate the speed of a swimmer who swims 100 m in 56 s.
Step 1 What do you know? distance: d = 100m, time: t = 56 s
Step 2 What do you want to find? speed: s = d / t
Step 3 Substitute s = 100 m / 56 s, s = 1.8 m/s
